About TerraShield

Landslides, a frequent and devastating natural disaster in Malaysia, claim lives and cause millions in damages during monsoon seasons. Exacerbated by climate change, these events threaten both urban and rural communities, including remote tribal areas. To address this, TerraShield uses Machine Learning (ML) to predict landslide risks in Western Sarawak. By analyzing diverse datasets from NASA and other sources, we identified key variables driving landslides and developed a highly accurate ML model. This model powers a Landslide Susceptibility Map (LSM), visualized as an interactive 3D model to pinpoint vulnerable locations. With 99% accuracy in testing, TerraShield equips policymakers with a reliable tool to prioritize early prevention and mitigation, safeguarding communities and reducing economic losses.

Our model was developed using R "neuralnet" package with data from NASA (ALOS PALSAR DEM, Global Landslide Catalogue), ESRI (Land Use and Land Cover), FAO Soil Map, CHIRPS Pentad: Climate Hazards Center InfraRed Precipitation With Station Data (Version 2.0 Final), and EU soil map of Sarawak.

Project Metrics

Our model was evaluated through Area Under the Curve (AUC) where it evaluated to have an AUC of 0.99
